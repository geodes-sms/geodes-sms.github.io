Three papers from the GEODES group have been accepted at [ASE 2022](https://conf.researchr.org/home/ase-2022).

In the main track, the paper [AST-Probe: Recovering Abstract Syntax Trees from Hidden Representations of Pre-Trained Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.11719) was accepted, written by José Antonio Hernández López, [Martin Weyssow](https://martin-wey.github.io/), Jesús Sánchez Cuadrado, and [Houari Sahraoui](http://www.iro.umontreal.ca/~sahraouh/).

Two papers were also accepted in the ASE NIER Track:

Global Decision Making Over Deep Variability in Feedback-Driven Software Development, authored by Kienzle _et al._, including [Jessie Galasso](https://jgalasso.github.io/), [Houari Sahraoui](http://www.iro.umontreal.ca/~sahraouh/), and [Eugene Syriani](http://www-ens.iro.umontreal.ca/~syriani/) from the GEODES group.

and

[End-to-End Rationale Reconstruction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.00398), authored by [Mouna Dhaouadi](http://www-ens.iro.umontreal.ca/~dhaouadm/), [Bentley James Oakes](https://bentleyjoakes.github.io/), and [Michalis Famelis](https://michalis.famelis.info/).

Congratulations to all the authors!
